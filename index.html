Why Most Men Wait Too Long to Treat Hair Loss

Dr. Priya Menon · Dermatologist, MCI Registered
Medically reviewed · March 2026 · 8 min read

Male pattern hair loss affects approximately 58% of Indian men by age 50. Yet the average man waits 3–5 years after first noticing thinning before seeking treatment. That gap has measurable consequences.

The biology of delay

Hair loss is driven by dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a derivative of testosterone that binds to receptors in genetically susceptible hair follicles. Over time, DHT causes follicle miniaturisation — each growth cycle produces thinner, shorter hairs until the follicle stops producing visible hair altogether.

The critical insight: miniaturised follicles can be rescued. Dead follicles cannot. The earlier you intervene, the more follicles remain viable for treatment.

What the clinical data shows

Finasteride has been shown in peer-reviewed studies to reduce scalp DHT levels by approximately 60–70% at the standard 1mg daily dose. In landmark trials, approximately 83% of men maintained their hair count over 2 years, and about 66% saw measurable regrowth.

Minoxidil — a topical vasodilator — works through a different mechanism, increasing blood flow to the scalp. Studies suggest combining both treatments produces better outcomes than either alone.

The cost of waiting

Every month of delay means more follicles transitioning from miniaturised (rescuable) to dormant (much harder to treat). A 90-day protocol costing less than ₹1,299/mo is dramatically more affordable and less invasive than corrective procedures later.
